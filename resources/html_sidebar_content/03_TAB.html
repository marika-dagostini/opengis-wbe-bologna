MAE and MAPE

When referring to the entire study area, MAE and MAPE were defined as
\[ MAE_e = \frac{1}{T} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \left| y(t) - \hat{y}(t) \right| \]
\[ MAPE_e = \frac{1}{T} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \frac{\left| y(t) - \hat{y}(t) \right|}{y(t)} \]
where \( y(t) \) is the number of COVID-19 cases registered in the entire study area on day \( t \), \( \hat{y}(t) \) is the corresponding number predicted by the deterministic model, and \( T = 589 \) is the number of days in the study period. At the small-area level, we computed MAE but not MAPE, as MAPE requires dividing by the actual number of daily cases, which was frequently zero, making the percentage error undefined on those days. We defined MAE at a small-area level as
\[ MAE_{sa} = \frac{1}{N \cdot T} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \left| y_i(t) - \hat{y}_i(t) \right| \]
where \( y_i(t) \) is the number of COVID-19 cases registered in small area \( i \) on day \( t \), \( \hat{y}_i(t) \) is the corresponding number predicted by the deterministic model, and \( N = 724 \) is the number of small areas.

To facilitate comparison with the results reported by Fondriest et al. (2023) and to examine the model’s consistency across different spatial scales, we aggregated the small areas into 67 larger zones with at least 2,000 residents each, referred to as aggregated areas. We then computed MAE and MAPE at the aggregated-area level comparing the daily predicted number of COVID-19 cases with the observed cases smoothed using a 7-day Simple Moving Average (SMA). We refer to these as
\[ MAE_{sma} = \frac{1}{K \cdot T} \sum_{k=1}^{K} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \left| \tilde{y}_k(t) - \hat{y}_k(t) \right| \]
\[ MAPE_{sma} = \frac{1}{K \cdot T} \sum_{k=1}^{K} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \frac{\left| \tilde{y}_k(t) - \hat{y}_k(t) \right|}{\tilde{y}_k(t)} \]
where \( \tilde{y}_k(t) \) is the number of COVID-19 cases observed in registered area \( k \) on day \( t \) and smoothed using a 7-day SMA, and \( k = 67 \) is the number of aggregated areas.
